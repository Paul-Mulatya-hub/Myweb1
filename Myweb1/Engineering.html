Electricity Production Process

    Energy Conversion: These sources convert energy into a usable form. Dams use water flow to spin turbines, while power plants burn fuels to create heat for steam turbines. Wind turbines directly convert wind to rotational energy.

    Generator: The spinning turbines in power plants act as generators, creating electricity through electromagnetic induction.

    Transformer: The generated voltage is often too high for direct use. Transformers step-up the voltage for efficient long-distance transmission

    Transmission Lines: High-voltage electricity travels through a vast network of overhead power lines or underground cables.i

    Substations: At substations, transformers step-down the voltage to levels suitable for distribution.

    Transformers (Again): Finally, transformers near buildings further reduce the voltage to levels safe and usable for appliancess

    Consumption: Electricity reaches homes and businesses, powering lights, electronics, and machinery, completing the cycle.
